# Compare eDoc and ASiC-E Viewers

If you need to open or verify an eDoc or ASiC-E file, the right tool depends on what you are trying to do. Most people only need one of three options: a browser viewer, a desktop signing app, or an official validator.

## Quick Answer

- **Browser viewer**: best when you just need to open the file, inspect documents, and review signatures quickly.
- **Desktop signing app**: best when you need to sign documents, use smart cards, or handle recurring professional workflows.
- **Official validator**: best when you need an official or jurisdiction-specific validation result.

## Decision Table

| Option | Best for | Installation | Can sign documents | Validation style | Privacy profile |
|--------|----------|--------------|--------------------|------------------|-----------------|
| **Browser viewer** | Opening and inspecting received files | None | No | Practical document and signature review | Document contents stay local; live validation checks may still contact trust services |
| **Desktop signing app** | Signing, smart-card workflows, repeated use | Required | Yes | Full workflow tool | Mostly local, depends on the app and setup |
| **Official validator** | Formal or official verification flows | None | Usually no | Official or workflow-specific validation | Typically server-side |

## Which Option Should You Choose?

### I just need to open an eDoc file someone sent me

Use a **browser viewer**. This is the fastest option when you want to inspect the contents, identify who signed the file, and download the individual documents inside the container.

### I need to sign documents or use a national eID workflow

Use a **desktop signing app**. This is the better fit when your goal is not only viewing, but creating signatures, using dedicated signing hardware, or managing repeated signing tasks.

### I need an official validation result

Use an **official validator**. If a public authority, trust-service provider, or business process expects a specific validation result, use the validator or portal required by that workflow.

### I am on a work laptop, borrowed computer, or phone

Use a **browser viewer**. It avoids installation and is usually the simplest way to open a received `.edoc`, `.asice`, or `.sce` file.

### I work with large batches or regular signing tasks

Use a **desktop signing app** or the system required by your organization. Browser viewers are convenient, but they are not a replacement for a full signing workflow.

## Where eDoc Viewer Fits

eDoc Viewer is best when you want:

- Fast access in any modern browser
- No desktop installation
- Local handling of document contents
- Quick inspection of signatures, certificates, and timestamps
- A simple way to open `.edoc`, `.asice`, and `.sce` files

eDoc Viewer is not the right choice when you need:

- Document signing
- Hardware-token or smart-card workflows
- A validation result from a specific official portal or authority
- A system tailored to a mandatory enterprise workflow
